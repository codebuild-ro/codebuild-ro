## 🧠 How I work

- **Problem-first, not tool-first.** Most of my best work has been untangling the gnarly stuff — non-deterministic LLM behavior, retrieval quality, smart-contract security, gas costs — without derailing delivery.
- **Translator, not lone genius.** Leadership to me is bridging backend, frontend, and product, keeping everyone aimed at the real problem, and making room for experts to do their best work.
- **Honest about the edges.** Asking the right question and admitting what I don't know beats pretending to out-expert the room.

---

## 💭 On AI in engineering

> *AI is a force multiplier for expertise, not a substitute for it.*

It's widening the gap between senior and junior engineers more than it's flattening it: it amplifies people who can already judge a solution, and quietly piles up technical debt and security holes for those who can't. The leverage is real — but so is the need for the judgment to wield it.
